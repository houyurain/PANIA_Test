Welcome to PANIA

Alzheimer’s disease (AD) is a degenerative brain disease which affects about 5.7 million Americans, and the number will grow to about 13.8 million by mid-century. AD and related dementia (ADRD) is a multifactorial and heterogeneous disorder that is driven by a constellation of genetic and environmental risk and protective factors. Currently, drug discovery research has not been translated into effective treatments, requiring the community explore novel and alternative treatment methods. Non-pharmacological interventions (NPI), such as sleep, diet，dietary supplements, aerobic exercise, aromatherapy, light therapy, and cognitive training are potentially modifiable and thus offer alternative opportunities for AD/ADRD prevention. Multimodal NPIs have also been reported to be effective in improving cognitive function. However, the synergetic effects of pharmacological interventions (PI) and NPI on ADRD are largely unexplored. Biomedical literature contains much evidence of PI and NPI effects on diseases (including AD/ADRD) and associated gene targets and pathways, allowing us to further understand and discover NPI candidates and their synergistic with PI for AD/ADRD. However, computational tools to mine literature and discover novel PI and NPI for AD/ADRD are still lacking. Using both population-based data (e.g., electronic health records (EHR), Optum Clinformatics® data, clinical trials) and animal studies to re-rank and validate our hypothesis discovered from literature would enable us to identify most plausible PI/NPI candidates and their combinations for AD/ADRD.


Aim 1. Constructing a comprehensive Pharmacological And Non-Pharmacological Interventions for Alzheimer’s Disease Knowledge Graph (PANIA-KG) from biomedical literature and other knowledge bases. We will leverage our prior work to compile and expand an NPI ontology and develop natural language processing (NLP) methods to extract NPI entities and relationships with other biomedical entities from literature. We will integrate the extracted knowledge from literature with other existing knowledge bases to construct a comprehensive knowledge graph, PANIA-KG. The deliverables are NLP tools to extract NPI knowledge from biomedical literature and a comprehensive PANIA-KG integrating multiple knowledge bases.


Aim 2. Detecting, understanding, and visualization of drug repurposing signals of PIs, NPIs, and their synergistical effects for AD/ADRD using the PANIA-KG. Using the PANIA-KG, we will develop novel neural network-based link prediction methods to predict and rank individual PI and NPI candidates as well as the synergistic pairs of those top-ranked PI and NPI candidates (including PI/PI, NPI/NPI and PI/NPI combinations) on AD/ADRD. We will utilize visualization to increase the explainability of the prediction models and understand the underlying mechanism of the synergistic effects. We will deliver signal prediction methods and ranked lists of PI and NPI candidates as well as their combinations on AD/ADRD.


Aim 3. Re-ranking and validating individual and synergistical drug repurposing signals using multimodal data sources and animal models. We will use clinical trials to re-rank and large-scale EHR data to in silico validate individual drug repurposing signals (PIs or NPIs) generated in Aim 2. Furthermore, we will use mouse models to in vivo validate top ranked pairs of PI and NPI candidates. We will deliver the informatics methods for mining clinical trials and EHR and validated individual and synergistic PIs and/or NPIs.


Impact: This is the first application to develop a translational informatics framework to aggregate, standardize and discover the effects of PI and NPI candidates on AD/ADRD using multi-modal data resources (i.e., biomedical literature, EHR, clinical trials) followed by mouse model validation. The successful completion of this project will deliver a comprehensive NPI knowledge graph, novel informatics approaches, ranked list of PI and NPI candidates, and validated synergistic intervention using multi-modal data sources. The generated approaches, PANIA-KG and ranked lists can further our clinical investigations and clinical trial design which focuses on synergistic effects of PIs and NPIs for AD/ADRD.
